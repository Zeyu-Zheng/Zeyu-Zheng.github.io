Research Works

  1. Turán number of 3-graphs

    2. On the Turán number of the linear 3-graph C13
    The Electronic Journal of Combinatorics Volume 29, Issue 3 (2022), P3.46
    Chaoliang Tang, Hehui Wu, Shengtong Zhang, Zeyu Zheng
    We proved and strengthened a conjecture of András Gyárfás on the Turán number of a linear 3-graph by introducing an innovative double counting.

  2. Tournaments and kings

    3. Common kings of a chain of cycles in a strong tournament
    Logan Post, Zeyu Zheng
    We strengthened a well-known result by proving that each king in a strong tournament is a common king of strong subtournaments of any size, solving a problem proposed by András Gyárfás.

  3. Planar Turán number

    4. Extremal planar graphs with no cycles of particular lengths
    Ervin Győri, Xianzhi Wang, Zeyu Zheng
    We found a new and shorter proof to the planar Turán number of $C_5$, and a better-looking extremal construction.
    We developed a systematic technique to determine the maximal number of edges in a bipartite/triangle-free planar graph without small cycles.

  4. Graph rigidity properties

    5. Four-regular graphs with extremal rigidity properties
    Robin Huang, Tibor Jordán, Henry Simmons, Kaylee Weatherspoon, Zeyu Zheng
    We fully characterized minimum 3-edge rigid graphs. We proved some structural properties of lowly redundantly (globally) rigid graphs.
    We showed the sharpness of a lower bound for the minimum number of edges in a 3-vertex rigid graph.

  5. Small quasi-kernel conjecture (in progress)

    6. Yaobin Chen, Hehui Wu, Zeyu Zheng
    We are now working on the small quasi-kernel conjecture. Currently we have generalized a result of Alexandr Kostochka.

  6. Independent sets in hypergraphs (in progress)

    7. Bhargav Narayanan, Zeyu Zheng
    We are using the entropy method / occupancy fraction to study the number of independent sets in regular uniform hypergraphs.

  7. Twins in permutations (in progress)

    8. Zeyu Zheng
    We consider the length of twins in common positions from some random permutations. Currently we have generalized a result of Maciej Gawron to get an upper bound.
